# 37 Practices of a Bodhisattva — Master Catalog of Commentaries

*Root text: Gyalse Tokme Zangpo (1295–1369), "The Thirty-Seven Practices of All the Bodhisattvas" (rgyal sras lag len so bdun ma)*

*Compiled July 2026. Companion files: [[COMPENDIUM — Homage and Verses 1–7]] (deep per-verse notes) and [[COMPENDIUM — Verses 8–37 (scaffold)]].*

## How to use this catalog

Everything below is sorted by **how you'd actually use it**: root text translations first, then free full commentaries, then audio/video series, then books to buy. Each entry notes lineage flavor where it matters. Recommendations for your group are at the bottom.

## 2. Full commentaries you can read free, today

These are complete (or near-complete) verse-by-verse commentaries, no purchase needed:

- **H.E. Garchen Rinpoche** (Drikung Kagyu) — [37practices.info](https://www.37practices.info/commentaries/garchen-rinpoche/), from a 2001 Taipei teaching. Warm, practical, relentlessly points everything back to bodhicitta and the mind of hatred as the only real enemy. Also as a [PDF at ratnashri.se](https://www.ratnashri.se/Commentary.pdf) and [audio on archive.org](https://archive.org/details/pt09_teaching).
- **H.H. Dalai Lama** (1974, Bodhgaya) — [37practices.info](https://www.37practices.info/commentaries/commentary-by-dalai-lama/). Classic lamrim framing; vivid images (home as "a river of attachment"). A different, later HHDL teaching is on [Study Buddhism](https://studybuddhism.com/en/tibetan-buddhism/mind-training/commentaries-on-lojong-texts/commentary-on-37-bodhisattva-practices-the-dalai-lama).
- **Dr. Alexander Berzin** (Gelug) — [Study Buddhism, 5 parts](https://studybuddhism.com/en/tibetan-buddhism/mind-training/commentaries-on-lojong-texts/commentary-on-37-bodhisattva-practices-dr-berzin). Analytical, precise, modern examples (phones, email, politics). Part 1 covers homage–v4; part 2 covers vv5–9.
- **Tsenshap Serkong Rinpoche II** — [Study Buddhism, 9 parts](https://studybuddhism.com/en/tibetan-buddhism/mind-training/commentaries-on-lojong-texts/commentary-on-37-bodhisattva-practices-tsenshap-serkong-rinpoche-ii). Traditional Gelug oral style.
- **Khenpo Tsultrim Gyamtso Rinpoche** (Karma Kagyu) — [free ebook at dharmaebooks.org](https://dharmaebooks.org/thirty-seven-practices-bodhisattva/) (PDF + EPUB, trans. Suzanne Schefczyk). Emptiness-forward: every practice tied back to lack of true existence. Songs-and-analysis style.
- **Khenpo Sodargye** (Nyingma, Larung Gar) — [free ebook PDF](https://khenposodargye.org/content/uploads/2024/03/The-Thirty-Seven-Practices-of-a-Bodhisattva-20240401.pdf); also his longer classical commentary [The Excellent Vase of Nectar](https://khenposodargye.org/teachings/khenpos-classical-teachings/the-excellent-vase-of-nectar-a-commentary-on-thogme-zangpos-the-thirty-seven-practices-of-a-bodhisattva/). Scholarly but story-rich; strong on lay-practitioner application.
- **Ven. Thubten Chodron / Sravasti Abbey** (Gelug) — the [37 Practices archive](https://thubtenchodron.org/buddhism/03-mind-training/05-37-practices/) collects several complete series with transcripts/audio (see §3), plus a [free full commentary book PDF](https://thubtenchodron.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/37Practices_of_Bodhisattvas_2018.pdf) (Awaken Publishing 2009, free distribution). Down-to-earth, western-psychology-aware.
- **Bhakha Tulku Pema Tenzing** — "The 37 Bodhisattva Precepts" (1990) circulates free online (listed on [Rigpa Wiki](https://www.rigpawiki.org/index.php?title=Thirty-Seven_Practices_of_the_Bodhisattvas)).

## 4. Published books (purchase)

**The two anchors named in your launch plan:**

- **Dilgo Khyentse Rinpoche — *The Heart of Compassion*** (Padmakara trans., Shambhala 2007). Nyingma. Widely considered the deepest single commentary in English; luminous, uncompromising, saturated with devotion. [Shambhala page](https://www.shambhala.com/a-guide-to-the-thirty-seven-practices-of-a-bodhisattva.html) sells its catalog; book at any bookseller.
- **Ken McLeod — *Reflections on Silver River*** (2014). His full written commentary; contemporary, psychologically acute, no Tibetan scaffolding. [Goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/22051977-reflections-on-silver-river) · [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Reflections-Silver-River-Ken-McLeod/dp/0989515311).

## Recommendations for your group

**Your anchor pair is right.** Dilgo Khyentse (*Heart of Compassion*) for depth and devotion; McLeod (*Silver River* + free 2006 audio) for contemporary directness. They pull in usefully different directions — one saturates the verse in lineage, the other strips it to the bone.

**Free ecosystem around them, per verse:** Garchen Rinpoche and the Dalai Lama on 37practices.info, Berzin on Study Buddhism, KTGR and Sodargye ebooks, and Chodron's transcripts. That's six complete free commentaries — more than enough for any verse, forever.

**For handouts:** use the Pearcey/Lotsawa House translation (CC-licensed) and/or the Ruth Sonam free-distribution PDF. Avoid photocopying from the commercial books.

**Lineage spread**, if anyone in the group asks: Nyingma (Dilgo Khyentse, Sodargye, Penor), Kagyu (Garchen, KTGR, Karmapa, McLeod's Shangpa training), Gelug (HHDL, Berzin, Sonam Rinchen, Tegchok, Chodron). The text itself predates the schools' hardening and is claimed by all of them — a nice point for your first session.
